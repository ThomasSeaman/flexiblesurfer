Arm Circles

Stretching your arms and shoulders is always break after a good back workout. Starting from hips to back to shoulder will allow the tight connecting areas between areas really lose. Most of our issues our developed in the lower back or middle back. These key “in-between” areas that are hard to stretch. I like to start with small circles forward and backwards then move to larger circles and into moving to arms up and down opposite eaachother. This creates torsional stretches similar to long, efficient and balanced paddle strokes in the the water.

Puppy pose

Surfers can often develop poor overhead flection from not stretch their shoulders. Puppy pose is an awkward one on the beach prior to a surf so finding a wall or yoga mat to do this on after or before a surf can really benefit blood flow and overhead mobility. I often forget about this stretch but it is really good for preventing injury from a overhead impact. We have had taken a slam with a hand up in the air and felt that small twitch pain when lifting our hands over heads for the coming weeks. A nagging injury that you can feel paddling back out to the lineup.

Corba pose

Cobra pose posture is very similar to the position we are in prior to popping up on a way. The more strength, mobility and balance you have in this position the more likely you are to feel comfortable with a late drop in. Cobra pose will also help alleviate upper back tightness between the shoulder blades.

Theracane surf warmup

Now that we are stretch out it is important to work out any knots developed in the shoulders. I like to start with my traps. This can be a little painful but make sure to push hard and avoid any bones. I keep one theracane at the office and one next to my couch. Since they are so affordable it is nice to have a couple laying around for a mid day knot release. There isn’t an exact way to use it since everyones leverage points are a little different, don’t be afraid to get a little weird with it. I didn’t know what the hell it was the first time I looked at one of these.

Shoulder behind reach stretch

Now that the knots are clear and our shoulders are warmed up it is important to work on more mobility. Pull your shoulders behind your body, you will be amazed at how far you are able to pull yourself around after a couple weeks. It is important to have a warmed up back and shoulders prior to reaching far here.

Puppy pose on wall

Now we can go back to that puppy pose and use the wall. Push deep into this movement and work on driving your hands overhead.

Lacrosse Balls for Massage Therapy

Take to the wall as a cool down and put the ball of your should against the wall with the lacrosse ball. You will feel each tender get massaged as you more from the back on the ball of each shoulder. This is a really good exercise for small twitch pain or injuries. It will promote blood flow to the area.

Arnica Cream for Surf Shoulder Pain

In the event of an injury Arnica gel has been a saving grace. It reduces surface inflammation and can really easy pain directly after an injury.
